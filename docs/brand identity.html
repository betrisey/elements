EPFL Brand Identity

Corporate identity & logo

The EPFL logo contributes to the recognition and image of the institution: it must include ALL the letters of the acronym and full name, placed beneath it. Those two elements are inseparable.

Use on digital platforms

Any request to use the EPFL logo on a digital medium (website, mobile application, file of all kinds, newsletter, etc.) is subject to validation with Mediacom, EPFL press and communication service.

For all information regarding the web guidelines, please visit the atelierweb website (French only).

For the use of the EPFL logo in on printed material, please read the official guidelines.

Rules and recommendations

Exclusivity

To the exception of external partnership units or projects, the EPFL logo is the only official logo within the institution. No other logos or individual units within EPFL can be added.

Use of EPFL logo

The EPFL logo can be used on:

  • All written material (letters, brochures, leaflets, programs, flyers, reports, etc.)
  • All visual communication material (posters, slides, films, etc.)
  • All EPFL websites and pages (URL must include epfl.ch)

This rule is valid for all above-mentioned materials published by EPFL offical units and associations, as well as their partners in joint activities (eg. congreses, forums, conferences, seminars, lectures, academic or cultural events, etc.).

Logo placement

  • Letterhead, business cards: the EPFL logo must be placed at the top-right corner (contact the print center for more precise information);
  • Websites: the EPFL logo must be placed at the top-left corner (please read about the web digital identity).

For all other media, please respect the logo and its visibility.

Use by students associations

The use of EPFL logo by students associations is subject to certain terms and conditions. Please read about them on the DAF associations website.

Denominations: use of capital and lower cases

Ecole polytechnique fédérale de Lausanne
Only the word "Ecole" takes a capital letter.
In English, we refer simply to "EPFL", which is one of two Swiss Federal Institutes of Technology.

Units
When designating a specific unit, only the first word must take a capital letter (e.g. "the Faculty of life sciences", "the Section of mechanical engineering"). In more general contexts (e.g. one of the School faculties), the first word can be written with a lower-case letter.

Professor
If the title is quoted in a conference, "Professor" must be written with a capital letter.
When talking about professors in general, lower cases must be used.
